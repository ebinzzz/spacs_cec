Organization Roles and Responsibilities

Role Duties
CHAIRMAN
  • Provide overall leadership and vision for the organization.
  • Chair meetings and ensure efficient operation of the executive committee.
  • Represent the organization externally in a professional capacity.
  • Guide strategic planning and decision-making processes.
VICE-CHAIR
  • Support the chairman in all aspects of their role.
  • Act as chairman in their absence or as delegated.
  • Assist in developing and implementing organizational policies.
  • Coordinate with other officers to ensure smooth operation.
SECRETARY
  • Maintain accurate records of meetings and official correspondence.
  • Handle administrative tasks such as scheduling, communication, and documentation.
  • Ensure compliance with organizational bylaws and procedures.
  • Support the chairman and vice-chair in their duties.
JOINT-SECRETARY
  • Assist the secretary in maintaining records and administrative tasks.
  • Step in to fulfill the secretary's duties when needed.
  • Collaborate closely with the secretary and other officers.
  • Help organize and manage internal communications.
MEMBERSHIP DEVELOPMENT OFFICER
  • Develop and implement strategies to attract and retain members.
  • Coordinate membership drives and outreach efforts.
  • Respond to member inquiries and maintain member databases.
  • Work with other officers to enhance member benefits and engagement.
ASST. MEMBERSHIP DEVELOPMENT OFFICER
  • Support the membership development officer in their duties.
  • Assist in organizing membership events and campaigns.
  • Help maintain and update membership records.
  • Engage with prospective and current members.
EVENT MANAGEMENT OFFICER
  • Plan and execute organization's events and programs.
  • Coordinate logistics such as venue booking, catering, and equipment.
  • Manage event budgets and ensure events are within budgetary limits.
  • Evaluate event success and gather feedback for improvement.
ASST. EVENT MANAGEMENT OFFICER
  • Assist the event management officer in organizing events.
  • Handle specific event tasks as assigned.
  • Support event logistics and coordination efforts.
  • Ensure smooth execution of events.
MARKETING ADMINISTRATOR
  • Develop marketing strategies to promote the organization.
  • Create promotional materials and campaigns.
  • Manage social media channels and online presence.
  • Analyze market trends and adjust marketing strategies as needed.
CHIEF INFORMATION OFFICER
  • Oversee the organization's IT infrastructure and systems.
  • Ensure data security and manage information technology resources.
  • Implement technology solutions to support organizational goals.
  • Provide technical support and guidance to other officers.
PUBLIC RELATIONS OFFICER
  • Manage the organization's public image and relations.
  • Write press releases and handle media inquiries.
  • Develop and maintain relationships with media outlets and stakeholders.
  • Promote organization's activities and achievements.
ASSISTANT PUBLIC RELATIONS OFFICER
  • Support the public relations officer in their duties.
  • Assist in preparing communication materials.
  • Help manage social media platforms and communications.
  • Coordinate with other officers for public relations efforts.
TRAINING AND DEVELOPMENT OFFICER
  • Identify training needs within the organization.
  • Develop and implement training programs.
  • Evaluate training effectiveness and feedback.
  • Promote professional development among members.
ACADEMIC AFFAIRS COORDINATOR
  • Coordinate academic-related activities and initiatives.
  • Facilitate collaborations with educational institutions.
  • Organize workshops, seminars, and academic events.
  • Support members in academic pursuits.
SOCIAL MEDIA MANAGER
  • Create and curate engaging content for social media platforms.
  • Manage social media accounts and communities.
  • Monitor social media trends and analytics.
  • Respond to inquiries and engage with followers.
DESIGN TEAM LEAD
  • Lead the design team in creative projects.
  • Coordinate with other teams for design requirements.
  • Ensure quality and timely delivery of design projects.
  • Provide artistic direction and feedback to team members.
DESIGN TEAM CO-LEAD
  • Support the design team lead in their responsibilities.
  • Assume lead responsibilities in their absence.
  • Collaborate closely with team members on design tasks.
  • Contribute to design brainstorming and idea generation.
VOLUNTEER COORDINATOR
  • Recruit, train, and manage volunteers for organization's activities.
  • Assign tasks and oversee volunteer schedules.
  • Recognize and reward volunteer contributions.
  • Ensure a positive volunteer experience within the organization.
